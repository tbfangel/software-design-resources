# Dan North Overview

Dan North is a programmer, consultant, and the originator of Behavior-Driven Development (BDD). His writing spans agile software delivery, testing practices, organizational effectiveness, and software design principles. His work emphasizes practical approaches that prioritize business value over technical orthodoxy.

## Key Series & Themes

**Behavior-Driven Development**: North's most significant contribution, introducing BDD as an evolution of TDD that addresses communication gaps between technical and business stakeholders. The series includes foundational articles defining the Given-When-Then format, story templates, and ubiquitous language.

**Effectiveness Patterns**: A recurring theme examining what makes teams highly productive, including studies of exceptional teams, critiques of cargo-cult practices, and practical patterns for accelerating delivery.

**Deliberate Discovery**: An approach to planning and learning that acknowledges uncertainty, focusing on identifying and addressing knowledge gaps rather than pretending comprehensive upfront planning is possible.

**CUPID Principles**: North's proposed alternative to SOLID principles, emphasizing joy in coding through composability, Unix philosophy, predictability, idiomatic code, and domain-based design.

**Parables and Stories**: North frequently uses narrative devices (The Busy Kitchen, The Lady in the Taxi) to illustrate systems thinking and organizational dynamics.

## Recurring Themes

- **Value over process**: Consistently argues that software development exists to deliver business outcomes, not to perfect craft or follow methodologies religiously
- **Communication as core challenge**: BDD, ubiquitous language, and specification-by-example all address misalignment between stakeholders
- **Learning models**: Dreyfus model, Shu-Ha-Ri, and understanding how expertise develops inform his thinking on best practices and team development
- **Flow and throughput**: Lean thinking applied to software, minimizing lead time to business impact
- **Questioning orthodoxy**: Critiques of software craftsmanship, SOLID principles, and productivity metrics demonstrate willingness to challenge established wisdom
